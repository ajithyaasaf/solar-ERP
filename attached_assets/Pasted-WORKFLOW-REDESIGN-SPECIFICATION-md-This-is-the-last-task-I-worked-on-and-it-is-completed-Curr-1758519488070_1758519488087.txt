### Checkout Form Logic

Currently, the **site visit checkout form** handles both normal checkout and follow-up checkout.  

- In the **normal site visit checkout**, the outcomes are: **Converted**, **On Process**, or **Cancelled**. (This is correct.)  
- However, in a **follow-up checkout**, since the customer is already converted, the options should be:  
  **Completed**, **On Process**, or **Cancelled**.  
  This is more meaningful because it reflects the follow-up context.  

So, even though we use the same site visit checkout form, the outcome options should be adjusted dynamically depending on whether it’s a **normal visit** or a **follow-up visit**.

### Workflow Rules for Converted Customers

Now, let’s consider the case where a customer was already converted during the normal site visit and is in the **Completed** tab:  

1. I go to the **Completed** tab and start a follow-up for this customer.  
   - At this point, the customer should temporarily show in the **On Process** tab.  

2. After completing the follow-up visit, the customer should move back to the timeline and to the **respective tab** based on the final outcome:  
   - If **Completed** → He goes back to the **Completed** tab.  
   - If **On Process** → He stays in the **On Process** tab, because more work may be required.  
   - If **Cancelled** → The customer (with all his data) is moved to the **Cancelled** tab.
